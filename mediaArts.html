Homemade braised fish

    Homemade braised fish is my favorite dish. Every time I have my first meal home from abroad, my father will make this dish to entertain me. Once I got this dish and stared at the fish on the plate, wondering how it turned to be like this. Chinese people are relatively particular about the cooking procedure and the food ingredients are also complicated. It is impossible to imagine the original ingredients when each dish is served, which is different from western lettuce salad. Therefore, an idea came to me---deconstruct this dish. I am curious how the Homemade braised fish, which often appears on my dinner table, change from an original food ingredient to the appearance on the table? How to oobserve this favorite dish from a new perspective? I asked my father to collect all the food ingredients he used to make this dish for me. I cut these ingredients from various kinds of angles with a kitchen knife and tore wherever I could according to the texture. I found in the research that they all had unique textures and diverse colours inside. It could also present a new spatial order when they were put together. Four aspects were summarized to conduct in-depth study, which were respectively shape, space, colour and texture. I photographed the findings of these studies with a camera as well as reorganized them according to the new order and based on the four aspects. I start to observe the dish with a new rule and perspective but not a food taster. At last, I printed the research results into a brochure and put them into a food freezer bag. When the viewer opens the food freezer bag, what he see is not a dish but a artistic order presented by a familiar dish.
